Wildfire Management UAS System

Climate change and expanding wildland-urban interface (WUI) development are significantly increasing the risk of catastrophic wildfires in the eastern United States. This emerging threat underscores the critical need for advanced research into fire behavior within temperate hardwood forests and the integration of autonomous unmanned aerial systems (UAS) to enable safer, more effective wildfire management strategies.

At The Ohio State University's Laboratory for Autonomy in Data-Driven and Complex Systems (LADDCS), directed by Mrinal Kumar, we're developing autonomous drone technologies designed to prevent and mitigate wildfires while minimizing human risk exposure. My contributions to this initiative include:

  • Creating annotated masks for UAS thermal imagery analysis
  • Assisting in the assembly and configuration of UAS hardware systems
  • Contributing to the development of deep learning datasets for fire pattern recognition

Contributed to sensor system modeling, thermal sensing integration, and information architecture design

Co-author on upcoming research paper - Publication in final review process (Expected Q4 2024)
