Undergraduate Research Internship

This project investigated microstructural transformations and hardness evolution in maraging-like high-strength steels fabricated through Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF). It focused on how rapid thermal cycling and non-equilibrium solidification during metal additive manufacturing influence phase evolution, carbide precipitation, and mechanical properties. By characterizing reheated specimens using hardness testing and microstructural analysis, the study aimed to establish clear process–structure–property relationships for high-strength steels in additive manufacturing.

During my internship, I gained hands-on engineering experience operating lab equipment such as the Gleeble, polishing and cutting tools, cast pin tear testing setups, and optical microscopes. I developed proficiency in major software tools like Thermo-Calc and MATLAB, strengthened my knowledge in metallurgy, materials science, and welding, and built confidence navigating lab protocols and conducting independent research. This experience provided practical exposure to experimental design, data analysis, and scientific problem-solving in a materials engineering context.
