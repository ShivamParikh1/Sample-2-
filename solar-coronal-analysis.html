Solar Coronal Heating Mechanisms

This paper explores the mechanisms responsible for heating the solar corona, focusing on magnetic reconnection, Alfvén waves, and nanoflares. It highlights the role of coronal rain in both cooling and reheating the solar plasma, observed through instruments like the Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO) and Hinode.

The study emphasizes the diagnostic value of Fe XIII and Fe XIV emission lines in understanding temperature and density variations in the corona. Results show that these heating mechanisms are crucial for maintaining high temperatures, while coronal rain influences the energy balance, both cooling and reheating the plasma.
